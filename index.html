    Metric explanations

    What the metrics mean

    Token-wise log probability difference
    mean log P(positive tokens) - mean log P(negative tokens). Positive values indicate higher model probability for the positive sentence.

    SLOR (Syntactic Log-Odds Ratio)

    SLOR(sentence) = (LM sentence logprob - unigram sentence logprob) / number of scored tokens

    The unigram background distribution is estimated empirically from FLORES sentences using the same tokenizer as the model being scored (not an average of the model's own next-token distributions). Then:

    SLOR difference = SLOR(positive) - SLOR(negative)

    Different-token metric (only_diff_tokens_tokenwise_log_prob_diff)

    Example:

    Positive: I broke my arm .
Negative: I broke the arm .

    The aligned different subset is approximately positive: my / negative: the. Then:

    only_diff_tokens_tokenwise_log_prob_diff
    = mean logprob(positive different tokens) - mean logprob(negative different tokens)

    After-difference overlapping-token metric (after_diff_tokens_tokenwise_log_prob_diff)

    Using a similar example:

    Positive: I broke my arm yesterday .
Negative: I broke the arm yesterday .

    After the difference my vs the, the later aligned equal tokens arm, yesterday, . belong to the after-difference subset (this is not every token after the first different position -- only later equal aligned spans):

    after_diff_tokens_tokenwise_log_prob_diff
    = mean logprob(positive equal tokens after first difference)
      - mean logprob(negative equal tokens after first difference)

    Pairwise signed choice confidence (pairwise_signed_choice_confidence)

    The model (OpenAI or a local Hugging Face model) is asked to choose sentence A or B twice, with the presentation order reversed the second time. The chosen output-label ("A"/"B") token logprob is not sentence probability -- this metric converts each of the two votes to a confidence value (probability of that vote's chosen label) and signs it by which original sentence (positive/negative) was chosen. The two signed vote values are always averaged into the final signed choice confidence, whether or not the two orderings agree -- a value near zero can mean the votes disagreed, that both votes were invalid, or that magnitudes happened to cancel; it is never a separately imposed disagreement rule. A vote whose top-1 output does not resolve to exactly A/B contributes zero rather than raising an error, and its raw output remains inspectable in the detail panel/hover tooltip. This metric is never combined numerically with the local-LM/SLOR metrics above.
